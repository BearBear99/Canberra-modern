About Us

A community for Canberra's modern heritage.

We're a small volunteer organisation, but we believe small groups can move big things. Since our first public program in 2016, we've been connecting Canberrans with the buildings, streetscapes and landscapes that define our city.

Our Story

Who are we?

Canberra Modern is a volunteer-led organisation dedicated to raising awareness of Canberra's mid-century modern architecture and landscape. Through tours, talks and public programs, we connect the community with the city's built environment and champion the ongoing conservation of its modernist heritage.

We're artists, architects, historians, writers — and enthusiastic amateurs. What unites us is the belief that Canberra's post-war era produced some of the country's most experimental and important buildings, and that these deserve to be recognised, understood and preserved.

Our heritage

What's so special about Canberra?

Canberra is a young city with a rich architectural legacy, shaped by major development periods from the 1920s to the late twentieth century. Despite this, much of our mid-century modern heritage — particularly from the National Capital Development Commission era (1958 – 1989) — remains under-recognised.

During this time, influential architects contributed to a distinctive collection of residential and civic buildings, many of which are now at risk of being lost. Canberra Modern works to raise awareness and engage the community in preserving this important part of the city's identity.
